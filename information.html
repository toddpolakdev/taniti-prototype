Tourist Information for Visitors to Taniti

To make your trip enjoyable and hassle-free, here are answers to some frequently requested questions:

Frequently Requested Information:

  • Power Outlets: Taniti uses 120-volt outlets, the same as in the United States.
  • Alcohol Sales: Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking Age: The legal drinking age is 18, but it is not strictly enforced.
  • Language:
    • Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, making communication easier for visitors.
    • In rural areas, very little English is spoken, especially by older residents.
  • Healthcare:
    • Taniti has one hospital and several clinics, with many multilingual staff members available.
  • Crime:
    • Violent crime is extremely rare.
    • As tourism grows, there are increasing reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Travelers should take standard precautions.
  • Holidays: Taniti celebrates numerous national holidays, during which many tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed. Visitors are advised to plan accordingly.
  • Currency and Payments:
    • The official currency is the U.S. dollar, but many businesses also accept euros and yen.
    • Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and most businesses accept major credit cards.

Enjoy your visit to Taniti! Plan ahead, stay safe, and immerse yourself in all the beauty and culture the island has to offer.
