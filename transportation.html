Transportation

Getting to and Around Taniti

Taniti offers convenient options for reaching and exploring the island, whether you’re arriving by air or navigating its scenic locales.

Arrival Options:

- Most visitors arrive by air via Taniti’s small airport, which accommodates both small jets and propeller planes.

- The airport is currently undergoing expansion to welcome larger jets in the next few years, making travel to Taniti even more accessible.

- A small cruise ship also visits the island, docking in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week, offering travelers a unique way to experience Taniti.

Ground Transportation:

- Public buses operate in Taniti City daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m., providing a reliable and affordable way to get around.

- Private buses connect other parts of the island, ensuring access to popular destinations and local attractions.

- Taxis are readily available in Taniti City, offering convenient point-to-point transportation.

- For those who prefer to drive, rental cars can be obtained from a local agency near the airport.

- Bikes and helmets (required by law) are available for rent from various vendors, making it easy to explore the island’s scenic paths.

Walking and Exploring:

- Taniti City is flat and very walkable, perfect for strolling through its charming streets and taking in the local culture.

- Many tourists stay in the vibrant Merriton Landing area, which is easy to explore on foot and offers access to many shops, restaurants, and attractions.

Whether you’re flying in, cruising, or traveling around by land, Taniti ensures a seamless and enjoyable transportation experience.
