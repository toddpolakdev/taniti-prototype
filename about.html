About Taniti

Taniti is a hidden gem in the Pacific, a small tropical island that offers a diverse and captivating landscape despite its compact size of less than 500 square miles.

A Land of Natural Beauty

  • The island boasts a mix of sandy and rocky beaches, perfect for relaxation, exploration, and water activities.
  • A small but safe harbor serves as a hub for local fishing boats and visiting vessels.
  • The lush tropical rainforests are teeming with life, offering a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.
  • At its core lies a mountainous interior, crowned by a small, active volcano that adds an element of awe and wonder to the landscape.

A Proud and Resilient People

Taniti is home to an indigenous population of approximately 20,000 individuals. For centuries, the island’s economy thrived on fishing and agriculture, deeply rooted in traditional practices and the island’s abundant natural resources.

A Growing Destination

In recent years, tourism has emerged as a major contributor to the Tanitian economy, bringing new opportunities while preserving the island's rich culture and heritage. Visitors are drawn to the island’s unspoiled beauty, vibrant traditions, and the warm hospitality of its people.

Discover Taniti—a place where natural wonders and cultural treasures blend seamlessly to create an unforgettable experience.
